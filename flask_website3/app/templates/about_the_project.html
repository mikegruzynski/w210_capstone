About The Project

Anjali Kayal, Mike Gruzinski, Max Lee & Maura Cullen

The Project

The "Root Cellar" project is the final capstone project for Univeristy of Caifornia, Berkeley Masters of Information and Data Science. Our goal is to make an easy to use meal-plan and pantry to make nutitional eating easier. A more concsientous, data-driven approach to weekly menu planning and cart sourcing not only encourages a more data-drven nutritional effort but also can lead to less waste.

The concept of this platform was created by team leader, Mike Gruzinski, as a pitch for a capstone project in the Berkeley MIDS program. The project requirements are relatively sparse and allow the team to develop from scratch a Data Science product over the course of 12 weeks.

We hope this impact project impacts:

  • Enable consumers to efficiently find recipes and create meals that maximize nutrients and taste preferences based on items in their pantry
  • Reduce food waste by building a meal plan that optimizes utilization of ingredients in their pantry and supplements with e-grocery cart
  • Aid education and research as a tool to study how different diets and individual food plans alter when changing macro nutrients and quantities
  • Assist restaurants, caterers, and other industry players as a tool to create seasonal menus
  • Enable Health Care Practictioners to monitor patients nutrient intake and suggest potential options to redirect patients
